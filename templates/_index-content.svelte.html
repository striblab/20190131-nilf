Minnesota's missing workforce

February 28, 2019

In many ways, the American economy is doing very well, building upon upswings following 2009's great recession.

But some economic measures can be deceptive, in particular job numbers that bely growing numbers of Minnesotans and other Americans exlcuded entirely from the labor force.

Analysis of Bureau of Labor Statistics data shows disparties in labor force participation throughout Minnesota, especially in northern rural counties stretching through the Iron Range.

When removing those in prison or otherwise instutitionalized for various reasons, along with retirees and full-time students, the remaining number of Minnesotans not in the labor force reach 20 or even 30 percent in some counties.

Pine, Carlton and Itasca counties have the highest percentage of their adult populations outside the labor force. Unemployment numbers too are highest for Minnesota in the Iron Range.
